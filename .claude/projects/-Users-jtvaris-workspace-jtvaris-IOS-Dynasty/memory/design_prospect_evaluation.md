Prospect evaluation uses a dual system: physical attributes as NFL-scale numbers (measurable at combine), mental/position skills as letter grades (A-F) with accuracy dependent on scouting staff and number of reports.

**Why:** College players can't be directly compared to NFL players on mental/positional skills. Numbers would be misleadingly precise. Uncertainty creates interesting draft decisions.

**How to apply:**
- Physical: numbers after combine, directly comparable to NFL
- Mental/Position/OVR: letter grades, imprecise initially (B-C range), narrowing with more scout reports
- Rookies start below ceiling (70-85% of true OVR), top picks slightly higher
- Potential shown as verbal assessment ("Elite ceiling"), accuracy depends on staff
- Development takes 2-4 seasons toward potential
- ~5% chance of immediate-impact rookies from any round
